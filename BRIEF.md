# P303 — Mobile Experience Case Study

## Project Overview
This project explores a mobile-first symptom check-in experience for patients who need quick guidance on what to do next when they’re not feeling well.

The experience is designed specifically for mobile context: quick interactions, minimal typing, and clear next steps.

---

## User

**Primary User:**  
A patient managing their own health at home

**Scenario:**  
The user is experiencing symptoms (e.g., fever, fatigue, cough) and wants to quickly understand whether they should rest, schedule an appointment, or seek urgent care.

**Context of Use:**
- On their phone
- Possibly distracted or multitasking
- Limited time and attention
- Potentially anxious

---

## Problem

Patients often face uncertainty when symptoms appear. They may:
- Delay seeking care due to lack of clarity
- Overreact and seek urgent care unnecessarily
- Struggle with complex healthcare tools

---

## Goal

Design a mobile-first experience that helps users:

- Quickly input symptoms
- Answer simple follow-up questions
- Receive a clear recommendation

---

## Core Task

Help a user understand what to do next based on their symptoms.

---

## Key Flow

1. User starts symptom check
2. User selects symptoms
3. User answers follow-up questions (one at a time)
4. User reviews summary
5. User receives recommendation:
   - Self-care
   - Schedule appointment
   - Seek urgent care

---

## Design Principles

### Mobile-First
- Large touch targets
- Minimal typing
- One task per screen

### Clarity
- Simple language
- Clear actions

### Guided Experience
- Step-by-step flow
- No overwhelming forms

### Reassurance
- Calm, trustworthy tone
- Clear explanations

---

## Constraints

- Not a medical diagnostic tool
- Simplified logic for prototype
- Focus on UX, not clinical accuracy

---

## Success Criteria

- Flow works end to end
- Easy to use without instructions
- Suitable for healthcare context
